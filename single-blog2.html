Reflection 2

Tuesday October 13th,2020

Who I am Storytelling Reflection.

Who I am exercise is an activity where the subject is required to create an audiovisual reflection on his identity, and the most representative aspects that contributed to the formation of it.
Since I was a child, I have liked to reflect on my environment, how I am as a person and what I can do to improve. However, I don’t always have the opportunity to share this with other people. This exercise seemed to me an interesting way to not only connect on a deeper level with myself but also with my classmates and teacher who I could see from a more human perspective and, of course, learn from them.
Through this exercise I allowed myself to create a composition that represents my style and identity. I chose this style of storytelling, first because it represents my artistic legacy on the part of my father’s family, my grandmother especially, secondly because I find it fun to do and enjoyable to witness. I chose the slides with a Vintage theme because as I see it, the identity of a human being is formed since its years of yesteryear, and so clearly adding family memories made it the most personal and meaningful experience. My idea was to connect with the listener and that he or she felt comfortable in the process.
Thanks to this exercise I reinforced my love for these kinds of activities and noticed how some aspects of my context touch my emotions at a deeper level than ever I knew.
I believe that to grow it is necessary to be in constant evolutionary change. I am not the same person I was a month ago and I can also assure that I will not be the same next one. This exercise has shown me that I must have a little more confidence in myself and I think that something similar should be done by each person at least once in life.
